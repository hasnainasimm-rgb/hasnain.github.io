The real story

My job title is Enterprise Integration Engineer. What that actually means is: I am the person who figures out why System A refuses to talk to System B, usually at 2am, usually two days before a deadline, usually with three different teams all pointing at each other.

I have 4+ years of experience doing this professionally with IBM App Connect Enterprise, IBM API Connect, and IBM DataPower. Before that, I did it unofficially while pretending everything was fine.

My work is essentially enterprise matchmaking — connecting systems that were never designed to communicate, speaking multiple languages simultaneously (REST, SOAP, ISO 8583, ISO 20022, JSON, XML), and making sure the messages get delivered even when the infrastructure has opinions.

I've built integration flows for some genuinely interesting problems: Raast payment flows (Pakistan's real-time payment system), NADRA government APIs (national identity verification), self-service kiosk banking (so you don't have to talk to a human at the branch), and pensioner verification systems (making sure pensions go to the right people, which feels important).

I've connected: Temenos T24 core banking · Siebel CRM · Salesforce Marketing Cloud · WhatsApp Business · Kafka · MQ-based messaging · various government APIs.

On the technical side I work in ESQL, Java, OFSML and whatever format the receiving system demands that week.

Integration work sits at the intersection of systems, teams, and competing requirements. I'm comfortable navigating that — whether it means resolving conflicts between business and technical stakeholders, translating complex requirements into clean architecture, or keeping production systems alive under pressure. Mostly the last one.

This blog is where I document what I learn. The things that aren't in the docs. The gotchas that take three hours to find. The patterns that work and the ones that looked smart until they didn't. If something here saves you a debugging session, we're even.
